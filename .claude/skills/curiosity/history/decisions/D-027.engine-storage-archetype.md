# D-027 — Engine storage: archetype — 2026-05-11

**Scope**: engine

**Context**: The ECS storage experiment
(`experiments/ecs-storage-comparison/`) measured archetype,
sparsesetslice, and sparsesetgroup across five workload rows —
iteration baseline, multi-component query in two scenarios,
structural churn in three population models, attach/detach churn,
and mixed — at 1k/10k/100k entity scales over five sessions. The
cumulative analysis lives in the experiment README's Finding
sections (2026-05-04 through 2026-05-11). sparsesetmap was culled
at iteration-baseline fidelity (R-007) and is not part of the
final picture. With the mixed workload landed, the experiment's
question — *which storage strategy should the engine adopt first,
and on what evidence?* — has the data to answer.

**Decision**: The engine adopts archetype as its default ECS
storage strategy. sparsesetgroup is preserved as a per-system
option for subsystems whose component set is stable and whose
iteration profile matches a declarable owning group, if such a
case surfaces with measurement justification; it is not the
default.

**Reasoning**: archetype leads or ties on every measured workload
axis except declared-group iteration. At 100k:

- *Iteration baseline.* Tied with sparsesetslice within 1%
  (8.73 vs 8.67 ns/entity). sparsesetgroup leads at 7.87 ns/entity
  via its lockstep fast path.
- *Multi-component query.* Trails sparsesetgroup ~7% on
  `multi_full` (queried set matches the declared group). Leads
  `multi_partial` outright — sparsesetgroup pays its fallback-path
  tax (~17% behind archetype), sparsesetslice trails ~5%.
- *Structural churn* (post-D-026). Leads at every scale across all
  three population models. At 100k `structural_steady`: archetype
  184 ns/op, sparsesetslice 262 ns/op, sparsesetgroup 305 ns/op.
- *Attach/detach churn.* Leads ~30-40% at 100k (62.4 vs 88.4 vs
  87.6 ns/entity). Sparseset's structurally-expected win on
  column-independent component churn did not materialize: its
  per-call `s.columns[cid]` map lookup on Read/Write surfaces as a
  ~13ns/op tax that dominates the per-tick op mix.
- *Mixed* (the cumulative realistic-workload check). Leads ~8% at
  100k (14.21 vs 15.48 ns/entity). Tighter than attach/detach
  because mixed mutates via iterator (Get returns a pointer) and
  the map-lookup tax doesn't fire on the hot path.

Two structural properties matter beyond the numbers. First,
archetype has no "wrong query" failure mode — queries falling
outside any optimized fast path remain competitive. sparsesetgroup
trails ~17% on `multi_partial` because its iterator carries
fallback-path overhead its fast path doesn't. Second, archetype's
complexity is bounded to two abstractions (archetype management;
the ID-indexed locations slice per D-026), versus sparsesetgroup's
lockstep invariant which must be maintained across every declared
group column on every Spawn / Despawn / Attach / Detach that
touches a group-eligible component — a distributed invariant
that pays maintenance cost whether or not the iteration win fires
in any given frame.

**Alternatives**:

- *sparsesetslice.* Simpler implementation; no archetype-table
  management; columns fully independent. Rejected as default
  because after D-026's archetype `locations` pivot it doesn't
  lead on any measured workload axis, and its per-call Read/Write
  goes through a map-keyed column lookup that surfaces as a
  measurable per-call tax in attach/detach-heavy work. Caching the
  column reference at the call site could close most of that gap,
  but the typed call-site surface that change implies is its own
  design question not driven by present need.

- *sparsesetgroup.* Hybrid: sparseset's column independence plus
  per-declared-group lockstep maintenance. Wins iteration baseline
  by 7-13% and `multi_full` by ~7%. Trails archetype on every
  workload that doesn't reap the fast path: `multi_partial` by
  ~17%, structural by 5-15%, attach/detach within noise of
  sparsesetslice. The hybrid is genuinely useful when the engine
  has a hot, stable query that justifies the lockstep tax — but
  that's a system-specific bet, not a default. Preserved as a
  per-system option contingent on future evidence.

- *sparsesetmap.* Culled at iteration baseline (R-007). Strictly
  dominated by sparsesetslice — same shape, hash-keyed sparse
  mapping where slice-keyed wins on every workload class.

**Open questions left forward-looking**:

- *Entity allocator generations* — reuse-safety under the
  dense-ID scheme that archetype's locations slice depends on.
  See `concepts/engine/entity-allocator.md`.
- *Typed call-site ergonomics* — the experiment's `Storage`
  interface is type-erased for measurement. The engine's typed
  call sites haven't been designed; that surface choice is its
  own future decision.
- *Archetype-table memory growth beyond 100k* — unmeasured.
  Per-archetype page sizing and reclaim-on-shrink are
  forward-looking.
- *Threading.* D-024 settled deferred-mutation semantics for
  single-threaded access. Concurrent patterns remain
  forward-looking.
