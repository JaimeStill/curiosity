# D-024 — Storage deferral semantics: Reading A — 2026-05-08

**Scope**: engine

**Context**: The ECS storage experiment's `Storage` interface has
declared `Despawn(id EntityID)` and `ApplyDeferred()` from the start,
but left their semantic relationship open. Two readings were tractable
during the orientation discussion that opened the deferred-mutation
work:

- *Reading A* — `Despawn` / `Attach` / `Detach` always defer;
  `ApplyDeferred()` flushes the queue. Storage owns the command
  buffer. Structural mutation is never observable mid-frame.
- *Reading B* — `Despawn` / `Attach` / `Detach` execute synchronously.
  `ApplyDeferred()` exists for a higher-layer concern. The caller
  takes responsibility for not mutating during iteration.

The choice is load-bearing for the per-backend command-buffer shape,
the entity-to-column-membership lookup question for the sparse-set
backends, and the structural-churn workload's call shape.

**Decision**: Reading A. The `Storage` interface guarantees that
structural mutation (`Despawn`, `Attach`, `Detach`) does not take
effect when called — the operation is enqueued onto a per-backend
command buffer and applied during the next `ApplyDeferred()` call.
Iteration is safe at any point because no in-flight structural
mutation is observable.

`Spawn`'s deferral is left open at this checkpoint; that semantics
will be settled in the session that lands `Despawn` + `ApplyDeferred`
against actual call sites, not by predicted ergonomics.

**Reasoning**: The user's framing was decisive: "if we didn't orient
it towards that, I wouldn't understand the point of having
`ApplyDeferred()` in the first place." Reading A makes
`ApplyDeferred()` load-bearing on the contract rather than vestigial.
It also matches established ECS patterns — Bevy and flecs both treat
structural mutation as deferred during iteration — and pushes the
iteration-safety guarantee into the storage layer where it is
enforceable, rather than relying on caller discipline.

The ergonomic cost is that callers cannot `Despawn` and then `Read`
the same entity in the same operation; that is an acceptable
constraint for the engine's idiomatic frame loop, where structural
mutations queue during one phase and apply between phases.

**Alternatives**:

- *Reading B (immediate mutation).* Rejected: makes
  `ApplyDeferred()`'s purpose vague, pushes safety responsibility
  outward, and would let `Storage` callers structurally mutate during
  iteration in ways that silently break some backends —
  `sparsesetgroup`'s lockstep invariant in particular cannot survive
  arbitrary in-iteration mutation.

- *Hybrid (Spawn immediate, Despawn/Attach/Detach deferred).*
  Considered; not chosen at this checkpoint. The Spawn question is
  deferred to a session with concrete call-site evidence rather than
  predicted ergonomics. The hybrid remains possible if Spawn
  deferral measurably degrades workload ergonomics; that decision
  will be its own decisions-log entry if it lands.
