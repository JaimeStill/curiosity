# D-034 — Compaction operations for the history logs — 2026-05-13

**Scope**: project

**Context**: `history/decisions/` had grown to 33 entries (D-001–D-033) and `history/resets/` to 16 (R-001–R-016). Many decisions duplicated substance now codified in `SKILL.md`, the behavior files, `conventions.md`, or source code. The Reset Protocol declared both logs append-only with "files never edited in place." That discipline is load-bearing during normal sessions but precluded deliberate maintenance, leaving the logs to drift from current reality.

**Decision**: Introduce two named on-demand maintenance passes as recorded exceptions to append-only — a *decision compaction pass* and a *reset cull pass*. Protocol codified in `SKILL.md` under "Compaction Operations," with pointer sentences in the Decisions Log and Reset Protocol sections. Operational invocation note added to `.claude/behavior/execution.md` under "On-demand maintenance."

The decision compaction pass triages each decision into one of four buckets: Discard (superseded with clear evolution), Absorb (durable convention written into the appropriate skill/behavior/conventions file or source), Compact (point-in-time choice appended to a rolling `history/decisions/archive.md`), Retain (still load-bearing as a standalone file). The pass produces a reset entry with `Trigger: Decision compaction pass` as its audit trail.

The reset cull pass removes resets that no longer inform upcoming work, evaluated during planning. Survivors are renumbered from R-001. A one-line maintenance note at the top of the now-renumbered most recent reset records the operation.

**Reasoning**:

- **Single rolling `archive.md` for compacted decisions.** Grep-friendly, preserves the `D-###` prefix inline, keeps maintenance friction low.
- **Renumber surviving resets rather than keep gaps.** Resets are the most volatile context with no external citations to specific `R-###` IDs; renumbering keeps the directory clean.
- **Named exception over dropping append-only.** Preserves the trust-the-log property during normal sessions; compaction is the deliberate, recorded operation that mutates the log.
- **On-demand rather than scheduled.** Compaction is bookkeeping, not work that drives the project forward. Matches the volume of triage to current need.
- **Reset retention based on relevance to upcoming work, not on recency.** Recency is an emergent signal, not an axiom — a cull pass evaluates each reset against the upcoming work surfaced in planning.
- **Active contradiction-resolution during the compaction pass.** Clear evolution → discard the superseded entry. Unclear contradiction → pause and align with the user.

**Alternatives**:

- *Per-pass archive file* — rejected; proliferation of files.
- *Single replacement archive* — rejected; loses visible historical entries from the working tree.
- *Drop append-only entirely* — rejected; loses the trust-the-log property during normal sessions.
- *Keep R-### gaps after cull* — rejected; resets have no external citations, renumbering is the cleaner default.
- *"Most recent reset always retained" as an axiom* — rejected; retention should fall out of relevance to upcoming work.
