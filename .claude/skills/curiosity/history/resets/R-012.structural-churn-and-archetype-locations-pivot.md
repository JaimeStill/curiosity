**Workflow observations this session**:
- **Mid-session scope expansion was the right call.** Pre-pivot data
  identified a measurable, addressable cost (locations map at scale);
  in-session implementation + re-measurement closed the loop in one
  context window rather than carrying the optimization to a separate
  session and rebuilding context. Pattern: when a data-driven
  optimization opportunity surfaces during a measurement session, in-
  session execution preserves the analytical context that motivated
  it. The discipline that protects this pattern is *grounded in
  measured data, not anticipated cost* — premature optimization
  doesn't get this affordance.
- **User pushback on "we should test all the things" was the
  right call.** When asked whether structural workloads should also
  vary component shape (single-column variant + 3-column variant),
  the right answer was "defer until data motivates it" — three
  population models × three backends × three scales was already a
  saturated measurement matrix; adding component-shape as a
  dimension would have ballooned scope without addressing a
  measured question. D-025's revisit (wider component shapes) is
  named in the Finding's forward-looking section, available when the
  data motivates it.
- **Decision-log entries land at the session that produces them.**
  Earlier in the session, when the locations swap was framed as a
  next-session deliverable, the D-entry was deferred to that session.
  When the swap landed in-session, the D-entry landed with it. The
  rule: D-entries don't get pre-allocated for future work — they get
  written when the work they document is concrete and the rationale
  has measurement support.
- **Pre-pivot CSV files are not preserved when the same backend is
  re-run.** The `results/` directory holds post-pivot CSVs only;
  pre-pivot data lives in this Finding's tables. Reproducing
  pre-pivot data requires checking out the prior commit. This is
  acceptable for an experiment (CSVs are intermediate artifacts; the
  Finding is the durable record), but worth noting so a future reader
  doesn't go looking for missing CSVs.
