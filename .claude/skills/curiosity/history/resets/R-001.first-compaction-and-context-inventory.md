**Workflow observation this session**:

- **Two protocol refinements landed before triage began.** The
  first run of the compaction pass surfaced that the codified
  protocol (four buckets, rolling `archive.md`) did not match the
  user's mental model of what compaction is for. The corrected
  framing — Graduate or Cull, no archive, citations stripped —
  was substantive enough that it had to land in `SKILL.md` before
  any decision could be triaged under it. The companion
  refinement — that an inventory of the official-context surface
  is the prerequisite for Graduate decisions to be mechanically
  findable rather than judgment-grade — was added as a sibling
  deliverable. Both landed before triage. Both outlast this
  session.
- **The protocol works when the inventory exists.** With the
  *Context surfaces* section in place, every Graduate decision
  had a topically natural home that could be named without
  re-deciding the hierarchy each time. The pass moved quickly
  once both prerequisites were in place. Future passes should
  reuse this pattern: if the protocol or the inventory feels
  off, fix them first; the triage is downstream.
- **The proliferation guard fired twice.** D-007 and D-025 were
  Cull candidates not because they were contradicted by a
  superseder, but because manufacturing a home for them in
  official context would have produced trivia sections or
  experiment-specific notes in design. The guard is the discipline
  that keeps the topical hierarchy clean over time.
- **Reset cull pass aggressively discharged 18 resets.** Following
  the retention test as written, every R-001..R-018 had its
  integrations embodied in current official-context state and its
  retained items carried forward by this entry. None added unique
  forward-looking content beyond what survives in the surfaces and
  in this reset. The cull pass renumbered this entry to R-001 and
  added the maintenance note at the top of the body. The first run
  of the reset cull pass discovered that on a clean post-decision-
  graduation tree, the cull cleanly removes everything older than
  the surviving most-recent entry — an outcome the protocol allows
  but worth flagging in case it feels too aggressive on later
  passes.
