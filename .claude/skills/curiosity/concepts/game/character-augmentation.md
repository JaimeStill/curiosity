# Character augmentation (concept)

## Question

How does the player evolve their abilities over time, what does augmentation feel like in a game whose self is separable from its bodies, and how is that progression gated by the research stations the player builds? In particular: does augmentation augment the persistent self, the occupied vessel, or both?

## Constraints already locked in

- Building research stations supports gradually augmenting what the player is — augmentation is the *why* of the building loop (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- The player is a cybernetic being whose self is the consciousness that persists across vessel swaps (`design/game/premise.md` — Capsule, Player).
- Progression is gated by research stations, which are themselves gated by building, exploration, and what the player encounters in the world (`design/game/premise.md` — Player; Aspirational targets, Persistence).
- The pace of progression is contemplative; augmentation should accumulate at a pace that rewards attention rather than grinding (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- Augmentation interacts with vessel-embodiment (`concepts/game/vessel-embodiment.md`); coordinating the persistent-self vs. per-vessel split is part of the question this concept needs to settle.
