# Vessel embodiment (concept)

## Question

How does the player's identity exist independent of any particular vessel, and what governs the swap between vessels — what triggers it, what carries across the swap, and what does the player feel during it?

## Constraints already locked in

- The player is a cybernetic being whose self is not bound to any single body — a consciousness that occupies vessels rather than being one (`design/game/premise.md` — Capsule, Player).
- Vessels are crafted, occupied, and exchanged; each interacts with the world differently (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- The vessels available to the player shape which regions of the world are reachable — vessel access gates region access (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- Identity must persist across changes of body, and the swap should feel like a relocation of attention, not a context reset (`design/game/premise.md` — Aspirational targets, Embodiment).
- The pace of the experience is deliberate; vessel mechanics serve contemplative exploration rather than twitch urgency (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
