# Physics playground (concept)

## Question

How do physics, vessel mechanics, and world systems combine to produce gameplay that is intrinsically fun — playful, expressive, and rewarding to experiment with — beyond serving goal-driven progression?

## Constraints already locked in

- Mechanics that combine physics, the world, and vessels are intended to provide a fun playground of gameplay in their own right (`design/game/premise.md` — Aspirational targets, Play).
- Physics-and-vessel interaction must be expressive enough to be played with, not only simulated past (`design/game/premise.md` — Aspirational targets, Play).
- Vessels are crafted, occupied, and exchanged; each interacts with the world differently (`design/game/premise.md` — Player; `concepts/game/vessel-embodiment.md`).
- Physics is inner-tier and shares the inner-tier's hot-path commitments (`design/engine/runtime.md` — Inner-tier members).
- The contemplative pace allows time for experimentation; the playground depends on physics-and-vessel interactions being responsive and discoverable, not only correct (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- Playful interaction threads through the other game concepts (`concepts/game/vessel-embodiment.md`, `concepts/game/conflict-and-encounters.md`, `concepts/game/building-and-research.md`) rather than living separately; coordination across them is part of what this concept will need to settle.
