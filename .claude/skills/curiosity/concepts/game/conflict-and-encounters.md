# Conflict and encounters (concept)

## Question

How does conflict express in a game whose baseline is contemplative exploration — what kinds of adversaries oppose the player, what kinds of encounters does the game build toward (combat, puzzle, pinnacle confrontation), and what makes labyrinthine encounter spaces feel coherent with the larger world rather than self-contained mini-games?

## Constraints already locked in

- The world has elements that do not want the player to progress; conflict is systemic, not just incidental (`design/game/premise.md` — Setting, Player).
- The contemplative pace is the baseline; tension builds against it rather than replacing it (`design/game/premise.md` — Player).
- Encounters span fighting through, thinking through, and pinnacle confrontations within labyrinthine spaces (`design/game/premise.md` — Player; Aspirational targets, Adversity).
- Adversary expressions and encounter spaces should feel coherent with the contemplative baseline rather than bolted on (`design/game/premise.md` — Aspirational targets, Adversity).
- Difficulty challenges the player without punishing them; encounter design serves engagement and accessibility rather than mastery as ordeal (`design/game/premise.md` — Aspirational targets, Adversity).
- Conflict interacts with vessel-embodiment (`concepts/game/vessel-embodiment.md`): which vessel the player occupies shapes how they engage adversaries and encounter spaces.
- Conflict interacts with character-augmentation (`concepts/game/character-augmentation.md`): augmentation prepares the player for harder encounters, and encounters provide the pressure that makes augmentation matter.
